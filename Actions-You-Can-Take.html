What can we do?

Fishermen play a crucial role in reducing marine plastic pollution. While governments and organizations are responsible for large-scale solutions, individual and collective actions by fishermen can make a real and immediate difference in reducing plastic pollution in the sea. Small actions can make a big difference
1

Bring trash back to shore

One of the most important actions fishermen can take is to avoid throwing any plastic waste into the ocean. Even small items like sachets, bottles, and food wrappers contribute to marine pollution. Fishermen can bring a small reusable bag or container on their boats to collect trash and properly dispose of it when they return to shore.

2

Retrieve plastic caught in nets

Whenever plastic is caught in fishing gear, it should be removed and disposed of responsibly instead of being returned to the water.Another important action is to remove plastic that gets caught in fishing nets. Often, plastic debris becomes tangled in fishing gear. Instead of throwing it back into the water, fishermen should collect and dispose of it responsibly on land. This helps reduce future pollution and also prevents damage to fishing equipment.

3

Participate in coastal cleanups

Participation in coastal and marine cleanup activities is another powerful way fishermen can contribute. By joining community cleanups, they help remove existing plastic waste from beaches, mangroves, and shallow waters before it drifts further into the ocean.
