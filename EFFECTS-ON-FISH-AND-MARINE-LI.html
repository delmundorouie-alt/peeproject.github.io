Effects on Fish and Marine Life

Plastic pollution severely harms marine organisms, including fish, turtles, seabirds, and coral reefs. Many fish mistake plastic for food because it resembles small prey. When fish swallow plastic, it can block their digestive system, cause internal injuries, or lead to starvation. Some fish die immediately, while others become weak and unhealthy.

Impact on Coral Reefs:

Plastic debris can smother corals, block sunlight, and introduce harmful bacteria. When corals are damaged or die, fish lose their natural habitat and breeding grounds. As a result, fish populations decline.



Impact on Marine Ecosystems:

Ghost nets continue to trap and kill fish, turtles, and dolphins even when no one is using them. This is called “ghost fishing” and contributes significantly to marine animal deaths.

How about our fishermen?
Of course. Plastic pollution is not only an environmental issue. It is also an economic and social problem for fishermen.

ECONOMIC AND SOCIAL IMPACT

More time spent cleaning plastic from nets instead of fishing

Increased damage to fishing equipment

Lower income for fishing families

Less food security for coastal communities

Increased conflict over limited fish resources

Reduced fish catch due to declining fish populations
