Trivia and Facts

  • Plastic lasts for hundreds of years in the ocean.

    Most plastic does not decompose like natural materials. Instead, it slowly breaks down into smaller pieces over time. A single plastic bottle can remain in the environment for up to 450 years, meaning the waste we create today can still be polluting the sea long after we are gone.

  • Millions of tons of plastic enter the ocean every year.

    Scientists estimate that around 8 million tons of plastic leak into the worldâ€™s oceans annually. This comes from poor waste management, littering, floods, and waste from boats and coastal activities.

  • The Philippines is one of the top contributors to marine plastic pollution.

    Because of its long coastline, dense population, and limited waste disposal systems in many areas, a large amount of plastic waste from the Philippines ends up in rivers and eventually reaches the sea.

  • Fish often mistake plastic for food.

    Many fish and other marine animals cannot tell the difference between plastic and real food. When they swallow plastic, it can block their stomach, make them sick, or cause death.
